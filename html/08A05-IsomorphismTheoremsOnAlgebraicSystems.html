isomorphism theorems on algebraic systems


In this entry, all algebraic systems are of the same type; they are all O-algebras. We list the generalizations of three famous isomorphism theorems, familiar to those who have studied abstract algebra in college.

Theorem 1.

If f:A→B is a homomorphism from algebras A and B. Then

A/ker⁡(f)≅f⁢(A).
Theorem 2.

If B⊆A are algebras and C is a congruence (http://planetmath.org/CongruenceRelationOnAnAlgebraicSystem) on A, then

B/ℭB≅Bℭ/ℭ,

where CB is the congruence restricted to B, and BC is the extension of B by C.

Theorem 3.

If A is an algebra and C⊆D are congruences on A. Then

  1. 1.

    there is a unique homomorphism f:A/ℭ→A/𝔇 such that

    \xymatrix⁢&⁢A⁢\ar⁢[d⁢l][⋅]ℭ⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r][⋅]𝔇⁢&⁢A/ℭ⁢\ar⁢[r⁢r]f⁢&⁢&⁢A/𝔇

    where the downward pointing arrows are the natural projections of A onto the quotient algebras (induced by the respective congruences).

  2. 2.

    Furthermore, if k⁢e⁢r⁢(f)=𝔇/ℭ, then

    • –

      𝔇/ℭ is a congruence on A/ℭ, and

    • –

      there is a unique isomorphism f′:A/ℭ→(A/ℭ)/(𝔇/ℭ) satisfying the equation f=[⋅]𝔇/ℭ∘f′. In other words,

      (A/ℭ)/(𝔇/ℭ)≅A/𝔇.
