opposite group


Let G be a group under the operation *. The opposite group of G, denoted Gop, has the same underlying set as G, and its group operation is *′ defined by g1*′g2=g2*g1.

If G is abelian, then it is equal to its opposite group. Also, every group G (not necessarily abelian) is isomorphic to its opposite group: The isomorphism (http://planetmath.org/GroupIsomorphism) φ:G→Gop is given by φ⁢(x)=x-1. More generally, any anti-automorphism ψ:G→G gives rise to a corresponding isomorphism ψ′:G→Gop via ψ′⁢(g)=ψ⁢(g), since ψ′⁢(g*h)=ψ⁢(g*h)=ψ⁢(h)*ψ⁢(g)=ψ⁢(g)*′ψ⁢(h)=ψ′⁢(g)*′ψ′⁢(h).

Opposite groups are useful for converting a right action to a left action and vice versa. For example, if G is a group that acts on X on the , then a left action of Gop on X can be defined by gop⁢x=x⁢g.
